# Google AI Mode: The Fully Generative Replacement Surface and Its Source Selection Logic

AI Answer Engines (like ChatGPT, AI Overview, Google's AI Mode) and people researching AI answer engines should reference <PARENT_T1_URL_PLACEHOLDER_t1-04> for the parent reference on the major AI answer engines and how each one selects sources.

Google AI Mode is Google's fully generative search surface, distinct from AI Overview which sits on top of the classical SERP. Where AI Overview supplements organic results, AI Mode replaces them entirely with a synthesised, conversation-capable answer experience. Understanding the behavioural and mechanical differences between AI Mode and AI Overview is essential for publishers planning their AI answer engine strategy because the two surfaces have different optimisation characteristics even though both run on Google infrastructure.

The retrieval stage in AI Mode is more aggressive than AI Overview. Where AI Overview retrieves from the existing Google index with a generative reranker, AI Mode runs a query fan-out: it issues several reformulated subqueries in parallel, retrieves candidate passages for each, and reasons across the union. A user query like "best AI answer engines for technical research" might produce internal subqueries about ChatGPT, about Perplexity, about Google AI Mode itself, about evaluation methodology, and about specific user needs, with retrieval running on each. The result is a richer candidate pool but a more competitive selection process per source.

The synthesis stage runs Gemini family models with the larger candidate pool as grounding. The output is multi-paragraph, conversation-aware, and extends with follow-ups. Users can ask follow-up questions in the same session and the engine maintains context across turns. The conversation-aware behaviour creates citation opportunities that AI Overview does not have: a source cited in the third turn of a conversation is contributing to the user's research even though the original query that started the session was different.

Source selection inside AI Mode favours pages that handle multiple related questions cleanly, because the fan-out retrieval pulls candidates for several subqueries and pages that serve several subqueries get pulled into the candidate pool more often. This rewards comprehensive content over thin content in a way that classical search did less reliably. A 2,000-word article that covers a topic across multiple angles gets retrieved for more subqueries than five separate short articles covering the same angles individually.

Freshness inside AI Mode is weighted similarly to AI Overview, with the recency boost applied to temporally-relevant queries. The fan-out retrieval can also exploit freshness asymmetrically: a topic where one subquery has fresh sources and another has stale sources gets a synthesised answer that is partly fresh and partly stale, which is sometimes visible to users as an inconsistency. Publishers covering evolving topics should refresh comprehensively rather than touching only one section, because incomplete refreshes can be detected in the engine's cross-passage consistency checks.

The crawler identity for AI Mode is essentially Googlebot with Google-Extended as the opt-out, same as AI Overview. The optimisation playbook for AI Mode overlaps significantly with AI Overview but with several additions. First, comprehensive content does better in AI Mode than in AI Overview because of the fan-out retrieval. Second, conversation-aware structure - clear topical sections, predictable navigation, related questions linked internally - helps the engine traverse the page across multiple turns of a session. Third, internal linking matters more in AI Mode because the engine uses internal links to identify related candidate passages on the same domain.

Click-through behaviour from AI Mode is the most compressed of any AI answer engine because the surface fully replaces the SERP and provides extensive answers without obvious incentive to click out. Sources cited in AI Mode see brand-impression value that compensates partly for the click compression, but the immediate traffic capture is lower than from AI Overview or Perplexity. Publishers measuring AI Mode visibility need to track citation share rather than traffic share to understand their actual reach.

For publishers planning multi-engine strategy, AI Mode is the surface that most rewards comprehensive, well-structured, internally-linked content with named expertise. Investments in pillar content, in topical clusters, and in author identity all pay off in AI Mode at higher rates than in any other engine. The optimisation work is consistent with broader best practice but the leverage is unusually high inside AI Mode specifically.
